### POST L-02 · The Methodology Post
**Type:** Education / Process

---

The hardest thing to explain about Signal Desk is why preservation matters as much
as verification.

Here's the problem:

A piece of evidence exists today. You screenshot it. You write it up.
Tomorrow the page is deleted. The account is suspended. The filing is amended.

Now your entire finding rests on a screenshot you took on your phone.

That's not evidence. That's a memory.

At Signal Desk, every piece of evidence is archived before analysis begins:
— Web page archived via Wayback Machine or archive.today
— Timestamp and archive URL logged to the evidence record
— Hash fingerprint generated for the preserved file
— Provenance notes added: how it was found, what tool was used, when

This isn't bureaucracy. It's the difference between a finding that holds up
and one that collapses the moment someone challenges it.

If you're building a compliance file or a journalism investigation and you're not
preserving as you collect — you're building on sand.

#OSINT #Compliance #DueDiligence #EvidenceStandards #OpenSourceIntelligence

### POST L-03 · The Confidence Ratings Post
**Type:** Education / Differentiator

---

The thing most research desk reports don't tell you:

How confident are you, really?

Signal Desk assigns a confidence band to every major claim in every report:

**HIGH:** Confirmed by 2+ independent, preserved, verified sources.
No contradicting evidence. Direct support.

**MEDIUM:** Supported by one or two credible sources.
No contradicting evidence, but incomplete corroboration.

**LOW:** Single source, unverified provenance, or significant uncertainty.

**UNRESOLVED:** Cannot be assessed with current evidence.
Question remains open.

This matters for two reasons:

1. For compliance teams: a confident-sounding report with weak evidence is a liability,
not an asset. You need to know what's actually proven.

2. For journalists: the difference between "confirmed" and "one source says" is
the difference between a publishable finding and a legal risk.

We write both on the tin. Always.

#DueDiligence #Compliance #OSINT #InvestigativeJournalism #RiskManagement

### POST L-06 · Case Type: Corporate Due Diligence

The most common brief we receive:

"We're about to do a deal. Tell us what we should know about this company and
its principals before we sign."

Here's what a Signal Desk corporate due diligence brief covers:

**Beneficial ownership:** Who actually controls this company?
Through what structure? Any PSC register gaps?

**Type:** Trust / Process

The most important part of our process isn't the AI agents.
It's the step where a human reads everything and decides whether to release it.

Every Signal Desk report must pass:

✓ QA review (every factual claim checked against evidence)
✓ Legal review (defamation, privacy, safety, jurisdiction)

That last step is not a formality.

It exists because automated research pipelines — however good — can still:
— Miss context a human would catch
— Underweight a legal risk
— Produce something technically accurate but operationally harmful

The human gate is where we catch those things.

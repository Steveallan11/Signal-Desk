Key Findings
CLAIM-001
Andrej Babiš used Blakey Finance Ltd — a British Virgin Islands shell company established via Panamanian firm Alcogal — to inject $22 million for the acquisition of Chateau Bigaud in 2009. Funds were structured as a back-to-back loan routed through BVI, Washington D.C., and Monaco.
EVD-001 · EVD-002 · EVD-004 · 3 independent sources
HIGH
CLAIM-002
The corporate structure constitutes a "loan-back" scheme — a method described by former financial crime investigator Kamil Kouba as "textbook." The structure allows funds to appear as a legitimate loan while concealing beneficial ownership and potentially reducing tax liability on capital gains.
EVD-001 · EVD-004 · Expert testimony on record
HIGH
CLAIM-003
None of the shell companies or Chateau Bigaud appeared in any of Babiš's mandatory Czech political asset declarations after he entered politics in 2013, despite Czech law requiring full disclosure of all properties and assets.
EVD-005 · Official Czech government records via Investigace.cz
HIGH
CLAIM-004
France's Central Office Against Corruption, Financial and Fiscal Offences (OCLCIFF) opened a formal money laundering investigation into Babiš in February 2022, examining whether the back-to-back loan was used to launder the proceeds of tax fraud. The investigation remains ongoing.
EVD-003 · Le Monde / ICIJ reporting
HIGH
CLAIM-005
SynBiol, a subsidiary within the Agrofert conglomerate controlled by Babiš via trusts, declared acquisition of the Monaco entities in its 2018 financial statements. The companies were merged into a SynBiol subsidiary by 2020.

Operation Bigaud — Offshore Structure Analysis
SUBJECT: Andrej Babiš · Chateau Bigaud · Mougins, France · $22M acquisition 2009
Executive Summary

Between 2009 and 2020, a multi-jurisdictional shell company structure routed $22 million through entities in the British Virgin Islands, Washington D.C., and Monaco to acquire Chateau Bigaud in Mougins, France. None of the entities or the property were disclosed in mandatory Czech political asset declarations.

Key Findings (excerpt)
Corporate chain: Blakey Finance Ltd (BVI) → DC entity (name unconfirmed) → SCP Bigaud (Monaco). Structured as a back-to-back loan.
HIGH EVD-001 · EVD-002 · EVD-004
Chateau Bigaud absent from all Czech asset declarations post-2013. French OCLCIFF probe opened February 2022. Status: ongoing.
